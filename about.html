About Me

Hello! I’m Noah and I am currently a first-year computer science major and mathematics minor at the University of Maryland, College Park. My journey into the computer science world first began my junior year of high school. My parents had always told me, “No matter what your career may be, it is always nice to know how to program”. Taking this to heart, I began to learn Java, my first programming language. Throughout the next year, I would build a solid foundation for programming as I became more familiar with different data structures and algorithms. Since the end of winter this year, I have been expanding my knowledge into some of the subfields within computer science. Recently, I have been learning web development, hence this website! Learning HTML, CSS, and JavaScript have given me more than just tools to use; they have contributed to my understanding of how our world works, and the many things we use everyday that we take for granted, such as the internet. As I have become more familiar with the different computer science fields, I have taken a big liking to machine learning and artificial intelligence. Without machine learning, the world as we know it today would be very different. We would not have the proper tools to protect against hackers and many of the technologies known today would not exist. Despite this, we have yet to scratch the surface of what machine learning can do for society. In the future, I would not love anything more than contributing to this field and providing modern solutions to modern problems.
